CBC

Components of CBC

Result Meaning Normal Values Significance
Blood Cell Counts Total number of cells RBC: 5; WBC: 4.5-10; Platelet: 150-450
Hb Concentration grams Hb / 100mL whole blood 15
Hematocrit Percent of whole blood that is RBCs (aka "packed cell volume") 45%
MCV Volume of RBCs 80-100 <80 = microcytic; >100 = macrocytic
MCHC Hb Concentration in packed cells Low MCHC indicates hypochromic anemia (not commonly used)
MCH Absolute amount of Hb per RBC Not that important

Notes:

  • hematocrit ~ Hb concentration x 3
  • hematocrit and Hb concentration are higher in men than women
    • androgens stimulate RBC production

Peripheral Smear

Examination of smear of blood on a microscope slide, allowing for observation of morphology

  • Poikilocytosis => irregular shape
  • Anisocytosis => irregular size
  • Polychromasia => variations in RBC staining
    • Hypochromic cells (decreased staining) => Hb deficiency in each cell

Reticulocyte Count

Reticulocytes are immature RBCs in the marrow (1 day) and circulation (1 day) that contain polyribosomes.
Reticulocyte counts assess the bone marrow's response to anemia.

  • Normal Reticulocyte Count = 1%
Cause of anemia Expected Change in Reticulocyte Count
Bleeding or hemolysis (RBC loss or destruction) Increased
Inadequate production substrates (Iron/Folate deficiency, Vitamin B12 deficiency) No increase
Bone Marrow Failure No increse

Corrected Reticulocyte Count - retic count x (patient hematocrit / normal hematocrit)

Differential WBC Count

Neutrophils > Lymphocytes > Monocytes > Eosionphils > Basophils

Bands - immature neutrophils with simple nucleus

  • Increased with acute bacterial infection => "left shift" (acute increase in number of bands)

Red Cell Distribution Width

Distribution of the MCVs of different RBCs. Increased red cell distribution width can be caused by:

  • Early anemia - early formed cells are abnormal in size
  • Reticulocytosis - retics have larger MCVs
  • Post-transfusion - different sizes with multiple populations of cells.
