Thalasemmia

Beta Thalasemmia

Mutations that result in absence of or reduction in amount of Beta globin chain

  • Manifests several months after birth due to gradual shift from gamma globin to beta globin gene expressions
  • Underproduction of beta chains => excess alpha chains
    • Insoluble alpha chains aggregate and oxidize RBC membrane => accelerated apoptosis of RBC precursors, hemolysis
Class Lab Findings Clinical Manifestations Genotypes
Trait / Minor Mild to minimal microcytic anemia, target cells, possible basophilic stippling, elevated HbA2 Mild anemia that can be confused with iron deficiency B/B+ or B/B0
Intermedia May require transfusions, bone disease, splenomegaly Microcytic anemia Many different (B+/B+ or B+/B0)
Major (Cooley's) Moderate to severe microcytic anemia in first 6 months of life, target cells and anisopoikilocytosis, nucleated red blood cells, transfusion dependent, can't make HbA => elevated HbF, no HbA on electophoresis, iron overload (low hepcidin) BM hypertrophy and extramedullary hematopoiesis, gallstones from chronic hemolysis B0 homozygous or B0/B+ with very low B+ activity

Treatment:

  • RBC transfusion therapy
  • Splenectomy
  • Iron chelation for iron overload
  • Stem cell transplant => cure

Alpha Thalassemia

Deletion mutations of alpha genes that decrease alpha globin chain synthesis => excess beta globin chains, which are soluble

Class Deletions Clinical Manifestations Lab Findings
Silent carrier 1 Usually clinically silent
Minor 2 Microcytic, hypochromic anemia, normal electrophoresis Mild anemia
Hb H Disease 3 Microcytic, Hb H inclusion bodies visible on cresyl blue stain, Hb H band on electrophoresis Chronic hemolytic anemia, transfusion dependent, splenomegaly, gallstones
Hb Bart's (gamma 4) 4 80% Hb Bart, no Hb F Hydrops fetalis - severe anemia, hepatosplenomegaly, HF, and extramedullary hematopoiesis in utero, pre-eclampsia in mother

Treatment:

  • Alpha typically requires no treatment
  • Hb H disease: Transfusion support, splenectomy, iron chelation
  • Hydrops fetalis: almost always lethal in utero

Note: Hb H disease clinically similar to beta-thalassemia intermidia
