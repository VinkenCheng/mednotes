RBC Function and Metabolism

Hemoglobin Synthesis

Requiresglobin chain + (Protoporphyrin IX + Fe++) .
Ferrochelatase (aka heme synthase) puts the Fe in protoporphyrin.

Porphyrias

Defective synthesis of protoporphyrin IX => skin rash, liver disease, neurologic disorders. Lead can also disrupt protoporphyrin synthesis.
Defect in:

  1. ALAS (early enzyme) => porphyria + sideroblastic anemia
    • Ringed sideroblasts with iron-laden, prussion blue staining mitochondria in BM, iron overloaded state
    • Iron enters mitochondria to form heme, but not enough protoporphyrin
    • Causes:
      • Alcoholism => B6 deficiency (B6 is a necessary cofactor for ALAS)
  2. Other enzymes later in the pathway => porphyria without anemia

Hemoglobinopathies

Point mutations or deleteions in alpha or beta globin locus => abnormal Hb protein. Hb comes from two different chromosomes:

  • alpha locus: chr. 16
  • beta locus: chr. 11

Under normal conditions:

  • A large portion of Hb at birth = Hb F (alpha2 + gamma2)
    • Hb F is "left-shifted" compared to Hb A, meaning it exchanges oxygen more efficiently at the hypoxic levels where oxygen exchange occurs in the placenta.
  • After infancy, majority of Hb is Hb A (alpha2 + beta2) and small amounts of Hb A2 (alpha2+delta2)

RBC Membrane Proteins

  • Mutations in spectrin, AE1 (band 3), ankyrin, 4.2 => hereditary spherocytosis
  • Mutations in spectrin, ankyrin, hub proteins => hereditary elliptocytosis

RBC Enzymes and Enzymatic Pathways

Embden-Meyerhoff Pathway

Aerobic glycolysis that generates 2 ATP per glucose and gives rise to other pathways.

Pyruvate Kinase deficiency causes a hemolytic anemia due to inadequate ATP production (pyruvate kinase needed for half of RBC ATP production)

Phosphogluconate Pathway - Pentose phosphate shunt

Generation of glutathione (GSH) via G6PD to protect cell from oxidative damage

G6PD Deficiency causes a hemolytic anemia that surfaces when patients are stressed with oxidants (sulfa drugs, fava beans)

  • X-linked recessive
  • Protective against malaria

Methemoglobin Reductase Pathway

Methoglobin reductase converts Fe3+ (which can't bind O2) to Fe2+ (metheme => heme) (ferric => ferrous)

Deficiency of methemoglobin reductase causes methemoglobinemia, a pathological buildup of methemoglobin unable to carry O2, leading to cyanosis

Leubering-Rapaport Pathway

Generation of 2,3-DPG, which causes a rightward shift in Hb dissociation curve and thus more O2 release to tissues

Consequences of Intravascular Hemolysis

Release of RBC contents:

  • K+ release => hyperkalemia
  • LDH release
  • Hb released into blood is bound by haptoglobin and taken up in the liver
    • If haptoglobin is exhausted, free Hb is toxic to renal tubules and binds NO => vasoconstriction => tissue ischemia
    • Protoporphyrin => bilirubin release
