Leukemias

Acute Leukemias

Acute Myeloid Leukemia

Neoplastic Proliferation of myeloblasts. Diagnosis requires >20% myeloblasts in bone marrow. AML is an oligoclonal disease.
General Features:

  • Older adults
  • Symptoms:
    • Fatigue, bruising, B symptoms, petechiae
    • Gum infiltration in monocytic AML
    • Sweet's Syndrome - myeloid luekemia cells infiltrate the skin and Chloromas (node-like masses under the skin)
  • Lymphoma Therapy can cause AML 5-15 years later
  • Peripheral blood smear appearance: blasts with Auer rods (crystal aggregates of myeloperoxidase), primary granules
  • Treatment:
    • APL - chemo + ATRA
    • Non-APL - induction chemo + consolidation chemo (no maintenance)

Acute Promyelocytic Leukemia (APL or AML M3)

  • t(15;17) => translocation of Retinoic Acid Receptor, PML blocks RAR
    • Blocks maturation of myeloblasts
  • Abnormal promyelocytes contain primary granules that build up and are released => DIC
  • Treat with all-trans-retinoic acid (ATRA), causing cells to mature and die
    • Can lead to cytokine storm (ATRA)

Acute Monocytic Leukemia

  • Proliferation of monoblasts that usually lacks MPO
  • Infiltrates gums

Acute Megakaryoblastic Leukemia

  • Proliferation of megakaryoblasts that usually lacks MPO
  • Associated with Downs syndrome

Acute Lymphoid Leukemia

A neoblastic proliferation of T cell or B cell precursors (lymphoblasts). Diagnosis requires >25% lymphoblasts in bone marrow.
General Featuers:

  • All lymphoblasts are TdT+, therefore TdT staining is used to identify them
  • More common in children. Younger than 1 or older than 30 => worse prognosis than 1-30
  • Symptoms: bruising, fatigue, dyspnea, B symptoms, petechiae
  • Can cross blood barriers => CNS and Testes prophylaxis is necessary
  • Treatment:
    • Favorable prognostic factors => chemotherapy alone - Induction, consolidation, delayed intensification, 2 years maintenance
    • Unfavorable prognostic factors, relapsed disease => chemotherapy to induce remission, then allogeneic HSC transplant

Chronic Leukemias

Chronic Myeloid Leukemia

Neoplastic proliferation of mature myeloid cells.

Pathogenesis

  • t(9;22) => Philadelphia Chromosome => BCR-ABL fusion, causing overactive tyrosine kinase
  • Different proteins indicate different variants of CML/ALL that are caused by 9;22 transfusion
    |BCR-ABL Protein|Associated Disease|
    |---------------|------------------|
    |P190|ALL, 9;22 variant|
    |P210|CML|
    |P230|Neutrophilic CML|

Clinical Features

  • Older adults
  • Many are asymptomatic and routine CBC shows high WBC
  • Weight loss, fever, night sweats, early satiety (organomegaly)
  • Splenomegaly (indicates accelerated disease w/ possibility of transformation to AML (more common) or ALL ("blast crisis")

Lab / Pathology Findings

  • Very low Leukocyte Alkaline Phopshatase (LAP)
  • Peripheral blood smear:
    • Increased platelets and basophils
    • Leukocytosis (mostly neutrophils)
    • Myelocyte bulge
    • Micromegakaryocytes (less lobulated megakaryocytes)
  • Over time, progresses to fibrosis
  • PCR (more sensitive) and FISH probe shows BCR-ABL fusion (9;22)
    • Repeated PCR is used to monitor BCR-ABL during treatment

Treatment

Imatinib: blocks tyrosine kinase activity

Chronic Lymphocytic Leukemia

Neoplastic proliferation of naïve B cells that are CD5 and CD20 positive. B cells are unable to undergo apoptosis

Pathogenesis

  • Monoclonal proliferation => elevated kappa OR lambda light chain
  • Leads to autoimmune disorders:
    • Hemolytic anemia
    • ITP
    • Agranulocytosis
    • Hypogammaglobulinemia - infection = most common cause of death

Clinical Features

  • Older adults, most common adult leukemia
  • Slow progression
  • Can involve lymph nodes (small lymphocytic lymphoma)
  • High risk of secondary skin cancers
  • Associated with Autoimmune Disorders
  • Other symptoms are similar to CML

Lab/Pathology Findings

  • Peripheral blood smear: Smudge cells, increased lymphocytes
  • Thrombocytopenia indicates late stage disease
  • Absolute Lymphocyte Count > 10,000 is required for diagnosis
