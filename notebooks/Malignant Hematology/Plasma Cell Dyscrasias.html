Plasma Cell Dyscrasias

General Principles

  • Gammopathy: any medical condition that produces an abnormal distribution of plasma immunoglobulins
    • Monoclonal: "M-spike" in blood or urine, typically whole Ig or light chain only (not just heavy chain), produced by clonal population of mature B cells
      • Causes: MGUS (most common) > Multiple Myeloma > AL Amyloidosis
    • Polyclonal: results from chronic inflammation (sarcoid), infection (TB), autoimmune disease

Monoclonal Gammopathy of Undetermined Significance (MGUS)

Benign, asymptomatic neoplasm of plasma cells

  • M-spike in serum or urine: IgG more common than IgA; IgM and light chain rare but possible
  • 1 percent / year progress to Multiple Myeloma
  • Increasingly common in elderly
Type of MGUS Progresses To:
IgG, IgA Multiple Myeloma
IgM Waldenstrom Macroglobulinemia
Light chain (kappa or lambda) Light Chain MM, AL amyloidosis, Light Chain deposition disease

Waldenstrom Macroglobulinemia

Lymphoplasmacytic Lymphoma

  • Lymphoma-like Disease: Hepatosplenomegaly, Lymphadenopathy
  • Always involves bone marrow
  • Secretes monoclonal IgM (big) => causes hyperviscocity
  • No bone lesions, no increased Calcium, but renal impairment and amyloidosis is possible

Plasmacytoma

A solitary malignant neoplasm of plasma cells in the bone marrow, not distributed in mutliple locations

  • Can be either within bone marrow or outside of bone marrow (extraosseous plasmacytoma)

Osteosclerotic Myeloma

  • Neoplastic proliferation of plasma cells that induces sclerotic changes in adjacent bone marrow => osteosclerosis
  • Occurs in patients with POEMS syndrome: Polyneuropathy, Organomegaly, Endocrinopathy, M-protein, Skin changes
  • Almost always lambda light chain

Monoclonal Immunoglobulin Deposition Disease (MIDD)

  • Deposition of whole Ig, heavy chain, or light chain, which can lead to disease
    • Deposition of IgL (light chain) can cause AL amyloidosis
      • Amyloid can bind Factor X => coagulopathy (raccoon eyes)
    • Deposition of Ig in the kidney => excess extracellular matrix secretion => cellular dysfunction or death => tubulopathy and cast nephropathy in distal convoluted tubule

Multiple Myeloma

A malignant plasma cell dyscrasia typically preceeded by MGUS

  • 1% of all cancers, twice as common in blacks
  • 10% of hematologic malignancies
  • Older adults (median age = 65)

Clinical Presentation and Findings:

  • Symptoms caused by End Organ Damage - CRAB:
    • Hypercalcemia - due to bone lesions
    • Renal Insufficiency
    • Anemia - replace normal cells in marrow (myelophthisic)
      • Mott cells in bone marrow: plasma cells with spherical inclusions in cytoplasm
      • Other cells can also be affected (platelets, granulocytes)
    • Punched Out Bone lesions - due to activation of osteoclast RANK receptors
      • Serum ALP is decreased or within normal limits, as opposed to other osteolytic bone disease
  • Can present with recurrent infections due to functional hypogammaglobulinemia
    • Infection is most common cause of death in MM
  • Biomarkers of Malignancy:
    • More than 60% plasma cells in Bone Marrow
    • Free Light Chain Ratio (FLC) > 100: indicates overproliferation of one type of light chain
    • One or more focal lesion on MRI

Differentiating MGUS, Smoldering MM, and MM:
