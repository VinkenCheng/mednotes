Lymphomas

Hodgkin Lymphoma

Neoplastic proliferation of Reed-Sternberg Cells

Epidemiology

  • Bimodal age distribution
  • Strongly associated with EBV, especially Mixed cell and Lymphocyte Depleted subtypes
  • More common in younger patients than Non-Hodgkin

Staging

  1. Only one lymph node
  2. More than one lymph node on same side of diaphragm
  3. More than one lymph node plus involvement on both sides of diaphragm
  4. Involvement of a non-lymphatic organ

Clinical Features

  • Enlarged but painless node
  • Cytokines secreted by Reed-Sternberg cells can result in "B symptoms":
    • B symptoms = fever, chills, weight loss, night sweats
    • In particular: Fever > 101 w/o explanation, >10% weight loss in last 6 months w/o explanation
    • B symptoms confer worse prognosis
  • Other possible symptoms include pruritis, alcohol intolerance
  • Spreads to contiguous nodes, commonly in lower neck and supraclavicular nodes

Pathology - Reed-Sternberg Cells

  • Large B cells w/ multilobed nuclei and prominent nucleoli => "owl-eyed nuclei"
  • CD15+ and CD30+
  • CD45-

Subtypes

  1. Nodular Sclerosis: most common, RS cells present in lacunae, visible sclerosis of lymph node on biopsy
  2. Lymphocyte-Rich: Best prognosis
  3. Mixed Cell: Associated with abundant eosinophils due to IL-5 production
  4. Lymphocyte-Depleted: Worst prognosis, associated with HIV and elderly

Treatment

ABVD - adriomycin, bleomycin, vinblastine, dacarbazine

  • Early stage => 2-4 rounds + radiation
  • Late stage => 6-8 rounds

Complications: pulmonary fibrosis (bleomycin), heart disease (adriamycin), thyroid dysfunction (radiation)

Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma

Proliferation of lymphoid cells that forms a lymph node or extranodal mass.
Can be divided into T cell neoplasms and B cell neoplasms.

  • All B cell subtypes are translocations involving chr 14 because that's where the Ig heavy chain gene is located
B Cell Size Subtypes
Small Follicular, Mantle Cell, Marginal Zone, Small lymphocytic lymphoma (i.e. CLL)
Intermediate Burkitt
Large Diffuse Large B Cell

Follicular Lymphoma

  • Proliferation of small CD20+ B cells that form follicle-like nodules => painless lymphadenopathy
    • Distinguished from reactive follicular hyperplasia by disruption of normal architecture, lack of macrophages, BCL2 expression, and monoclonality
  • Adults
  • t(14;18) => BCL2 overexpression => inhibition of apoptosis => proliferation
  • Can progress to diffuse large B-cell type
  • Treat with chemotherapy + rituximab (anti-CD20 Ab)

Mantle Cell Lymphoma

  • Proliferation of small CD20+ B cells in lymph node mantle
  • Older males
  • t(11;14) => cyclin D1 overexpression => G1/S transition facilitated

Marginal Zone Lymphoma

  • Proliferation of small CD20+ B cells in marginal zone of lymph node
  • Associated with autoimmune disorders: Sjogren syndrome, Hashimoto's thyroiditis
  • Gastric MALT lymphoma is a marginal zone lymphoma in gastric MALT
    • Often caused by H. pylori, and may regress with treatment of H. pylori

Burkitt Lymphoma

  • Proliferation of intermediate CD20+ B cells associated with EBV
  • Children or young adults with jaw or abdomen mass
  • t(8;14) => c-myc overexpression (oncogene)
  • High mitotic index (very aggressive), starry sky appearance on biopsy

Diffuse Large B Cell Lymphoma

  • Proliferation of large CD20+ B cells that grow in sheets
  • Most common form in adults
  • High-grade, clinically aggressive

Clinical Picture of Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma

Grade Typical Presentation Treatment
Low Elderly patient with slow growing nodes that may wax and wane, no B symptoms Varies from watchful waiting to chemo-immunotherapy; early stage may be cured with radiation
Intermediate Adults with nodes enlarging at worrying rate, +/- B symptoms Stage I or II: R-CHOPx4 and local radiation; Stage III or IV: R-CHOPx6
High Young adult or child with rapidly enlarging nodes, acutely ill with B symptoms, and high LDH due to high cell turnover Hyper-CVAD for Burkitts; Patients are hospitalized for treatment, often require transfusions, and have high chance of fever from neutropenia. CNS prophylaxis also given

Note: R-CHOP = rituximab, cyclophosphamide, Hydroxy-D (adriamycin), oncovin (vincristine), prednisone
